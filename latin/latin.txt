# Latin

# Vocabulary

Object of a preposition must be accusative or ablative.

# Nouns

The five declensions of nouns is determined by the ending of the genitive singular.

The accusative singular of masculines and feminines ends in -m and the accusative plural in -s, e.g., dominam, dominum, dominas, dominos

The dative and ablative plural are always the same, e.g., dominis.

The nominative, accusative, and vocative of neuter nouns are the same and in the plural end in -a, e.g, pilum, pila.

The vocative is the same as the noinative except for the second declension, e.g., domine.

## First Declension

First declension nouns end in -ae in the genitive singular.  For example, domina, -ae.  They are feminine, unless they denote males, e.g., nauta.

The nominative, genitive, and accusative cases in Latin correspond to the nominative, possessive, and objective cases in English.

nom domina bona
gen dominae bonae
dat dominae bonae
acc dominam bonam
abl domina bona
nom dominae bonae
gen dominarum bonarum
dat dominis bonis
acc dominas bonas
abl dominis bonis

base (domin-) + termination (-a)

But deabus and filiabus for dat and abl plural instead of -is.

Filia agricolae nautis fugam nuntiat.  The farmer's daughter announces the flight to the sailors.
Filia agricolae est grata dominae.  The farmer's daughter is pleasing to the lady.

## Second Declension

Second declension nouns end in -i in the genitive singular.  For example, dominus, -i.  They are masculine or neuter for -um nouns, e.g., pilum.

nom dominus bonus
gen domini boni
dat domino bono
acc dominum bonum
abl domino bono
nom domini boni
gen dominorum bonorum
dat dominis bonis
acc dominos bonos
abl dominis bonis

nom pilum bonum
gen pili boni
dat pilo bono
acc pilum bonum
abl pilo bono
nom pila bona
gen pilorum bonorum
dat pilis bonis
acc pila bona
abl pilis bonis

base (domin-) + termination (-us)

The vocative is domine.

## Predicate Noun

Predicate noun agrees in case with the subject of the verb, e.g., Lesbia est ancilla.

# Adjectives

Adjectives and noun agree in gender, number, and case.

Attributive adjective
Puella parva bonam deam amat.

Predicate adjective
Puella est parva.

# Verbs

Third person singular active ends in -t and the third person plural in -nt.  For example nauta pugnat, nautae pugnant.

# Word Order

Normal word order is subject + modifiers of the subject + indirect object + direct object + adverb + verb.  For example, filia + agricolae + dominae + pecuniam + non + dat.
